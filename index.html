Abstract

Removal processing is a method of shaping an object into the desired shape by removing material, like cutting vegetables with a knife. Shape transitions have a complex relationship with process conditions, such as material properties. Therefore, data collection cannot be executed in simulation and is limited in the real world; data collection costs are high due to its irreversibility.
This study proposes a data-efficient model-based reinforcement learning approach for robotic removal shaping operations. The proposed method focuses on the cutting surface in the removal process. It models the removal shape transition by decomposing it into two parts: geometric shape transition model based on the cutting surface independent of the process conditions and a model compensating for the shape deviation caused by the process condition. With those models, the proposed method achieves 1)efficient learning with a small amount of data and 2)applicability to different initial and target shapes. The effectiveness of the proposed method is confirmed by rough grinding operations on a simulator and an actual robot, respectively.
